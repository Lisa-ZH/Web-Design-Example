Employment FAQs

When it comes to information, Lee Health has you covered. Below are a list of common questions & answers for candidates considering joining our Lee Health Family.

How Do I Apply?

Apply online any time using our Job Search page. Candidates needing assistance or computer access may also apply at our Employment Center:

Employment Center — 6630 Orion Drive, Ste 101, Fort Myers, FL 33912

Hours: 8 a.m. to 4:30 p.m. Monday through Friday

Application kiosks are also available for candidates to apply online at our hospital locations:

  • Health Park Medical Center – Cafeteria
  • Lee Memorial Hospital – Cafeteria
  • Gulf Coast Medical Center — Cafeteria
  • Cape Coral Hospital – Main Lobby

Click here for application instructions. (link opens PDF in a new window)

How long is the Lee Health application process? How many steps does it take while applying online?

Our online application & pre-screening process includes the following steps:

Step 1: Search for a job and review job description (five to 10 minutes)

Step 2: Complete online application (10 to 15 minutes)

Step 3: Respond to online pre-screening questions (up to five minutes)

What happens to my application once I apply?

Once you complete our online application process, your application and/or resume will be entered immediately into our applicant database. All applications will be pre-screened based on minimum qualifications and experience. Preference may be given to individuals who exceed the minimum job requirements.

The best-qualified candidates are selected for an interview based upon stated qualifications, experience, and background. If selected for an interview, you will be contacted by a member of our recruiting staff.

Lee Health is a drug/tobacco-free workplace. Pre-employment drug testing is required. We are also an equal opportunity employer.

How can I get technical support after hours?

If you have difficulty filling out the online application after normal business hours (i.e., in the evenings, during holidays, or on weekends), call our help desk at 239-343-7900. You can also send an email to hr@leehealth.org (link opens in a new window) to request help. Please provide your phone number and email address when you can or email, so we can easily contact you the next business day.

How can I get technical support during normal business hours?

Our normal business hours are Monday through Friday, 8 a.m. to 4:30 p.m. If you require technical support while filling out your application during business hours, call HR Connect at 239-343-1555. You may also visit our Employment Center or one of our online application kiosks for personal assistance.

Are there technical requirements to apply?

For best results, we recommend that you use Google Chrome or Microsoft Edge when applying for a job at Lee Health

  • Internet Explorer is not supported.
  • We are mobile friendly, so use any device connected to internet to apply.

Please note Lee Health does not support, sponsor, nor endorse these browsers. These examples are just listed to give you other options to consider.

What types of careers are in health care?

There are hundreds of different career opportunities in health care. At Lee Health, our staff has numerous choices to meet their academic and financial needs. To learn about some of our career possibilities, visit our Job Search page.

How big are your hospitals?

Lee Health consists of four acute care hospitals: Lee Memorial Hospital, HealthPark Medical Center, Gulf Coast Medical Center, and Cape Coral Hospital, and two speciality hospitals: Golisano Children's Hospital of Southwest Florida and The Rehabilitation Hospital. At Lee Health, we see over 1 million patient contacts each year, making us the largest public health system in Florida.

Our staff includes more than 13,000 dedicated employees and 4,500 volunteers.

What is your nursing philosophy?

At Lee Health, we believe that providing highly skilled, competent, and compassionate nursing care is essential to fulfilling our commitment to excellence in clinical practice, theory, research, and community outreach.

Do you hire new graduate nurses?

Yes! We believe nursing professionalism includes mentoring the next generation. That's why we not only offer a variety of formal internship programs after graduation, but we also partner with local schools by providing experienced nurses as instructors for students during their nursing courses.

What is your nursing education department like?

Lee Health has developed an efficient yet comprehensive approach to meeting the educational needs of our nurses. We have unit-based Clinical Nurse Educators and Clinical Nurse Specialists. We partner with other departments to offer contact hour programs, American Heart Association certification, and nursing specialty certification reviews. We often present at national conferences, and it's always free for our employees to attend these programs.

We also provide the crucial first step in the education journey here at Lee Health with the hospital nursing orientation program that every new nurse attends. During this first step, an assessment is used to develop an individual orientation program.

Do you have internship programs?

Yes, internships are available in a variety of health care fields. Visit our For Medical Students & Residents page to see our ongoing programs in Nursing, Pharmacy, and Family Medicine.

What organizations is Lee Health affiliated with?

Lee Health is a member of Voluntary Hospitals of America, Florida Partnership, the Florida Hospital Association, and the Association of Voluntary Hospitals of Florida.
