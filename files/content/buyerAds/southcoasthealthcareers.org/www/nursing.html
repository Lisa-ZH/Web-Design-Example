Nursing at Southcoast Health

At Southcoast Health, we welcome nurses at every stage of their careers to join our dynamic team serving communities across Massachusetts and Rhode Island. With three acute-care hospitals — Charlton Memorial in Fall River, St. Luke’s in New Bedford, and Tobey in Wareham — as well as our Heart & Vascular, Cancer Care, Brain & Spine, Orthopedics and Health at Home programs, outpatient surgery centers, and multiple clinical specialties across 55+ physician practices and service locations, Southcoast Health offers a wide range of settings where nurses can grow and make a meaningful impact.

Our Values

At Southcoast Health our nurses are the backbone of our health system, embodying the values that define who we are.

Excellence. Our team strives to make Southcoast Health the best place to work and obtain health care.

Commitment. We demonstrate our commitment through our mission, goals and action.

Communication. We foster an atmosphere of open communication, mutual respect and trust.

Patient First. We work to exceed the expectations of our patients, their families and the community we serve.

Pathway to Excellence: Your Voice is the Pathway to More

Southcoast Health is committed to creating a positive, empowering environment for nurses—recognized through our Pathway to Excellence journey.

The Pathway to Excellence Program® is a premier nursing designation offered by the American Nurses Credentialing Center, the nursing excellence branch of the American Nurses Association.

This designation recognizes healthcare organizations with positive practice environments that promote nursing autonomy. This includes empowering nurses to make decisions about patient care, while also encouraging wellness and support for each other.

We are proud to be one of the first Pathway® designated hospitals in Massachusetts.

The Perioperative 101 RN Residency Training Program

Focuses on imparting the skills and proficiencies needed to thrive as a professional perioperative nurse. This six-month full-time paid program is focused on providing the knowledge and competencies required for the professional perioperative nurse. It welcomes experienced RNs with no operating room experience, new graduates, or operating room nurses returning after an extended absence. Current Southcoast Health employees are encouraged to apply.

Participants will learn the theoretical concepts, principles and skills to care for patients in the perioperative setting, using the recommended practices established by the Association of Perioperative Registered Nurses (AORN). The individuals who successfully complete the program will continue their nursing career at Southcoast Health in surgical operating rooms.

The first six weeks consist of the didactic portion of the program which includes classroom lectures, skills lab practice and observational experiences in the operating room. This will follow with clinical rotations to the circulating and scrub roles working with a preceptor in various surgical specialties.

“I applied for the perioperative program because I wanted to be a part of a team and make a difference in patients’ lives. The best part of the program, to me, was building connections with fellow students and supporting each other along the way. For anyone considering applying, I say go for it – especially if you’re looking to join a great team!” – Kyle Duarte, Perioperative 101 Residency Training Program 2025.
