Ready to further your education?

Through a generous grant from the Charles R. Wood Foundation, our MSN Nursing Education Pathway Program is intended to produce MSN graduates through SUNY Empire State University.

Upon successful completion, you will serve as a Clinical Instructor at your respective New York partner organization for two years.

What is the purpose of this program?

This program aims to significantly expand the pool of MSN-credentialed nurse educators, enabling them to provide clinical education for students entering or advancing in the nursing field. Graduates of this program will commit to a two-year dual service teaching agreement with a partnering New York State college of nursing.

Eligibility Requirements:

All candidates must:

  • Currently work at part-or full-time at AHMC or CVPH (per diem employees are not eligible).
  • Be in good standing with no corrective actions above a counseling level.
  • Possess a State of New York RN licensure
  • Possess an unencumbered license to practice as a registered professional nurse in the United States. This is an MSN program requirement.
  • Meet eligibility criteria for SUNY Empire (link opens in a new window). For additional information, connect with an admissions advisor at either institution.

Program Details

Offered in partnership with SUNY Empire State University, the MSN Nursing Education Pathway Program offers a comprehensive online curriculum while participants continue their employment within Champlain Valley Physicians Hospital (CVPH) and Alice Hyfe Medical Center (AHMC).

SUNY Empire has received generous funding from the Charles R. Wood Foundation to provide three partial scholarships for students enrolling in SUNY Empire’s MSN Nursing Education program. Additional funding will be contributed by CVPH and AHMC.

Upon completing the 36-credit MSN program at SUNY Empire, graduates will serve as clinical nurse faculty and work under a dual-service agreement for two years at a partnering New York college of nursing.

Visit the website of SUNY Empire State University (link opens in a new window) to learn more about their MSN program.

Program Costs and Commitments

Participants will be required to sign a retention agreement. Tuition costs will be covered by SUNY Empire grant funding and also UVM Health throughout the duration of the program.

Participants must commit to working as clinical nurse faculty at a partnering New York college of nursing for two years upon successful completion of the program (secondary interview required).

Frequently Asked Questions

When does the next cohort start?

Program participants selected will begin their studies in May 2026.

How many work hours are required during the program?

While enrolled in the program, participants will maintain their part or full-time employment status at CVPH or AHMC.

What if I’m a per diem or part-time employee? Am I eligible?

To be eligible, you must either be a part or full-time employee of CVPH or AHMC.

Which partners are included?

Recipients of SUNY Empire State University’s, Charles R. Wood grant include Alice Hyde Medical Center (AHMC) and Champlain Valley Physician’s Hospital (CVPH).

What if I’m not an employee at one of the Vermont UVM Health partners? Can I still apply?

Being an employee of UVM Health Vermont is a requirement for application and enrollment. To be eligible for the program you’ll need to be hired as a full-time employee in any Vermont UVM Health partner.

Interested in joining our team?

Call 800-722-9922 and choose option 3 to talk with one of our talent acquisition partners about job opportunities at Alice Hyde Medical Center (AHMC) or Champlain Valley Physicians Hospital (CVPH).

What if I am a Vermont resident, can I still participate?

Vermont residents are encouraged to apply if they currently work at CVPH or AHMC.

Since the grant only covers in-state tuition costs, out-of-state participants will be contacted to discuss their situation.

What is a dual-services agreement?

Participants will sign a two-year commitment to work as clinical nurse faculty at a partnering New York college of nursing upon graduation or while enrolled in this program. Participants must interview and gain selection at a partnering college or university. During the two-year commitment period, they will receive paid work-release.

I’ve started an MSN program in the past, but did not finish. Do I need to start over?

Participants will meet with admissions advisors to review past courses and grades on their transcript to determine whether they meet the requirements for the program.

Once I am enrolled in the Pathway Program, are there any tax implications that I should be aware of?

The amounts paid under this Program have tax implications. Amounts that are paid to your educational institution for tuition, fees, books, supplies, or other expenses constitute payments under an educational assistance program described in Section 127 of the Internal Revenue Code and are therefore excludable up to $5,250 per calendar year. Amounts paid in excess of $5,250 per year are taxable income and are subject to applicable withholding taxes.

These taxes will be withheld from your regular earnings, resulting in a reduction to your usual paycheck. To avoid unforeseen hardships, the reduction should be planned for, and if necessary, additional funding sources such as loans should be sought.

Please consult your personal tax advisor, as UVM Health cannot provide you with individualized tax advice.

I’m currently in an MSN program, can I apply to this program and transfer course credits?

Yes, participants will meet with admissions advisors to review past courses and grades on their transcript to determine whether they meet the requirements for the program.

Where can I get more information?

For more information, visit the websites for SUNY Empire State University:

  • SUNY Empire (link opens in a new window)

What happens if I am not selected for a seat?

This program is a one-time, grant-funded opportunity. If you are not selected, we encourage you to check this website regularly for updates on future programs.

If you are interested in pursuing your MSN independently, please reach out to your HR office to learn more about tuition reimbursement options that may be available to you.
