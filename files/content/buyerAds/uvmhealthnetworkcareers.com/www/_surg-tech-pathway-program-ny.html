Surgical Technologist (ST) Program (New York) at University of Vermont Health

Ready to further your education?

Through our unique partnership with Clinton Community College and Mohawk Valley Community College, our ST Pathway Program allows full-time employees of New York partners within the UVM Health to continue earning full-time wages and benefits while studying to complete their Associate of Applied Science in Surgical Technology degree and prepare students to sit for the National Board of Surgical Technology and Surgical Assisting (NBSTSA) national exam.

What does a surgical technologist do?

Surgical technologists are key members of the team that assists surgeries. They work closely with surgeons, registered nurses, anesthesiologists, and other hospital personnel. Surgical patient preparation and transport, operating room set up and the organization of surgical equipment are some of the surgical technologist’s duties.

Eligibility Requirements:

All candidates must:

  • Currently work at a New York partner site within the UVM Health.
  • Be in good standing with no corrective actions above a counseling level.
  • Have earned or obtained a high school diploma or GED by 6/30.
  • Meet Clinton Community College and Mohawk Valley Community College eligibility criteria. Eligibility requirements are subject to change. For additional details, visit the Mohawk Valley website (link opens in a new window) or connect with an admissions advisor.

Program Details

Offered in partnership with Clinton Community College and Mohawk Valley Community College, the ST Pathway Program offers a comprehensive curriculum that includes classroom learning, online education, hands-on labs, and clinical rotations within the UVM Health. While enrolled in the program, participants will work reduced hours in their current positions at UVM Health New York partner sites.

Students will be dually enrolled at Clinton Community College (CCC) and Mohawk Valley Community College (MVCC) throughout the duration of this program. Upon completing this 61-credit Associate of Applied Science program, graduates will be prepared to prepare you to sit for the National Board of Surgical Technology and Surgical Assisting (NBSTSA) national exam. Upon completion, students will work as surgical technologists at Alice Hyde Medical Center and Champlain Valley Physicians Hospital.

Participation requires full-time commitment across four semesters (plus summer) being offered online through MVCC and in-person classes and labs at CCC. Classes take place online through MVCC and at CCC’s Plattsburgh campus.

Visit the Mohawk Valley Community College website to learn more about their Surgical Technology Program. (link opens in a new window)

Program Costs and Commitments

Students who are selected for the program will be required to complete the requested FAFSA application to apply for state and federal tuition student aid. UVM Health partners will then cover tuition, fees, and textbooks for program participants after state, federal, and institutional aid have been applied.

Participants must work as a full-time ST (or alternative status if approved by leadership) at either Champlain Valley Physicians Hospital (CVPH) or Alice Hyde Medical Center (AHMC) for four years upon successful completion of the program.

Frequently Asked Questions

When does the next cohort start?

Program participants will begin their studies through Clinton Community College in the fall of 2023.

Please note that we are only planning on running one cohort starting in August 2023 and we have not made a decision to run additional cohorts at this time.

How many work hours are required during the program?

While enrolled in the Surgical Technology program, participants will work reduced hours per week in their current positions at UVM Health partner sites in New York. Participants will then receive payment for additional paid study hours to allow time for classwork, lab/clinical instruction, and study time.

How does tuition reimbursement work?

UVM Health partners will cover tuition, fees, and textbooks for select employees after state, federal, and institutional aid have been applied, in return for a four-year commitment.

What if I’m a per diem or part-time employee? Am I eligible?

In order to be eligible, you must be an employee of a partner within the UVM Health in New York.

What if I am a Vermont resident, can I still participate?

Vermont residents are encouraged to apply for the Surgical Technology Pathway Program (link opens in a new window) offered through Vermont Partner sites. Due to state licensing and accreditation requirements, our Surgical Technologist training programs are different in each state.

What if I am not an employee of the UVM Health, can I still participate?

We encourage you to contact Clinton Community College directly at admissions@clinton.edu (email link opens in a new window) if you are interested in enrolling in this program and are not employed by the UVM Health.

Why do I have to be enrolled at two colleges?

This innovative program allows Clinton Community College (CCC) to utilize Mohawk Valley Community College’s (MVCC) accredited ST curriculum and act as an extension of its campus. In turn, this helps our region quickly and efficiently gain access to educational pathways for critical occupations while allowing local residents and employees to learn in their own communities.

Program participants will be dually enrolled through Clinton Community College and Mohawk Valley Community College. Classes will be offered in person at CCC’s campus in Plattsburgh, while other classes will be offered online through MVCC or in person within Champlain Valley Physicians Hospital (CVPH).

Where can I get more information?

Please visit the Mohawk Valley Community College website. (link opens in a new window)
