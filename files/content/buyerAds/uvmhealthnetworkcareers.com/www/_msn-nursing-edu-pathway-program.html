Master of Science in Nursing (MSN) Nursing Education Pathway Program at University of Vermont Health

Ready to further your education?

Through a generous grant from the Vermont Agency of Health Services, our MSN Nursing Education Pathway Program is intended to produce MSN graduates through Norwich University and Vermont State University.

What is the purpose of this program?

This program aims to significantly expand the pool of MSN-credentialed nurse educators, enabling them to provide clinical education for students entering or advancing in the nursing field. Graduates of this program will commit to a two-year dual service teaching agreement with a partnering Vermont college of nursing.

Next Cohort - Important Dates

  • Network application deadline: November 3, 2023
  • Network notifications to employees: December 1, 2023 (for employees notified of their initial acceptance)
  • Norwich and Vermont State application deadline: December 8, 2023 (for employees notified of their initial acceptance)
  • Final Selections: January 3, 2024
  • Classes Begin at Vermont State: January 22, 2024
  • Class Begin at Norwich: March 4, 2024

Eligibility Requirements:

All candidates must:

  • Currently work at least 72 scheduled hours per pay period at a Vermont partner site within the UVM Health (part-time/per diem employees are not eligible).
  • Be in good standing with no corrective actions above a counseling level.
  • Possess an unencumbered license to practice as a registered professional nurse in the United States. This is an MSN program requirement.
  • Meet eligibility criteria for Norwich University (link opens in a new window) or Vermont State University (link opens in a new window). For additional information, connect with an admissions advisor at either institution.

Program Details

Offered in partnership with Vermont State University and Norwich University, the MSN Nursing Education Pathway Program offers a comprehensive online curriculum while participants continue their employment within the UVM Health.

The Vermont Agency of Health Services has generously provided grant funding for tuition, fees and textbooks for up to 25 UVM Health employees to attend the MSN programs at Vermont State (15 seats) and Norwich (10 seats).

UVM Health participants will receive an annual stipend/living allowance in order to continue earning full-time wages and benefits while pursuing their studies online.

Upon completing the 40-credit MSN program at either Norwich or Vermont State, graduates will serve as clinical nurse faculty and work under a dual-service agreement for two years at a Vermont college of nursing.

Visit the websites of Norwich (link opens in a new window) and Vermont State (link opens in a new window) to learn more about their MSN programs.

Program Costs and Commitments

Participants will be required to sign a retention agreement. Tuition costs will be covered by the Vermont Agency of Health Services throughout the duration of the program. Stipends will be covered by the UVM Health.

Participants must commit to working as clinical nurse faculty at a Vermont college of nursing for two years upon successful completion of the program (secondary interview required).

Frequently Asked Questions

Will this program be offered beyond the first cohort?

This is a one-time program being paid for by grant funds.

When does the next cohort start?

Program participants selected for the Vermont State University program will begin their studies in January 2023.

Program participants selected for the Norwich University program will begin their studies in March 2023.

How many work hours are required during the program?

While enrolled in the program, participants will maintain their full-time employment status at UVM Health Vermont partner sites.

Participants will receive an annual stipend/living allowance in order to continue earning full-time wages and benefits while pursuing their studies online.

What if I’m a per diem or part-time employee? Am I eligible?

To be eligible, you must either be a full-time employee of a Vermont partner site within the UVM Health, or be willing to become one.

Which partners are included?

Recipients of the Vermont Agency of Health Services grant include Central Vermont Medical Center (CVMC), Home Health and Hospice (HHH), Porter Medical Center (PMC) and UVM Medical Center (UVMMC).

What if I am a New York resident, can I still participate?

Yes, New York residents are encouraged to apply if they currently work at Vermont partner site within the UVM Health.

Since the grant only covers in-state tuition costs, out-of-state participants will be contacted to discuss their situation.

What is a dual-services agreement?

Participants will sign a two-year commitment to work as clinical nurse faculty at a partnering Vermont college of nursing upon graduation or while enrolled in this program. Participants must interview and gain selection at a partnering college or university. During the two-year commitment period, they will receive paid work-release.

I’ve started an MSN program in the past, but did not finish. Do I need to start over?

Participants will meet with admissions advisors to review past courses and grades on their transcript to determine whether they meet the requirements for the program.

I’m currently in an MSN program, can I apply to this program and transfer course credits?

Yes, participants will meet with admissions advisors to review past courses and grades on their transcript to determine whether they meet the requirements for the program.

Where can I get more information?

For more information, visit the websites for Norwich and Vermont State:

  • vermontstate.edu (link opens in a new window)
