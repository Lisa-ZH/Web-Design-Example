UVM Health Home Health & Hospice – Pre-Pathway Program (P3)

Ready to further your education?

Through our partnership with Community College of Vermont (CCV) our Pre-Pathway Program (P3) allows employees of UVM Health – Home Health & Hospice (HHH) to complete general education coursework. These courses are intended to prepare our employees for future academic pursuits in healthcare.

Upon successful completion, you may be eligible to apply to other Pathway programs within the UVM Health.

What is the purpose of this program?

This one-time, grant-funded, program aims to expand the opportunity for HHH employees to begin their education in healthcare at CCV. Courses will also provide a chance for employees to explore which specific healthcare careers may be of interest to them.

Program Details

Offered in partnership with Community College of Vermont the Pre-Pathway Program (P3) offers the opportunity for employees to begin their education in healthcare through general education coursework. Classes will also provide a chance for employees to explore which specific healthcare careers may be of interest to them.

Classes will be completed in addition to an employee’s regular work commitments. Students will not be paid for class time and are expected to maintain their scheduled work hours.

Classes will be paid for in full by UVM Health – HHH through a one-time grant opportunity for participants. Funding ends June 2025.

All HHH staff are encouraged to apply. HHH Human Resources matches the grant offer to include staff who pursue their career in non-HCBS healthcare pathways.

Classes that students may partake in must be related to furthering their healthcare careers. Healthcare careers encompass a wide range of professions that offer both clinical and non-clinical services to individuals within the community. These careers also involve roles that support the processes essential for delivering these services effectively. Specific courses will be determined on a case-by-case basis with students related to both academic readiness and need as well as future goals.

Participation in P3 does not guarantee admission into future UVM Health Pathway programs or at any other educational institutions.

Eligibility Requirements

All candidates must:

  • Currently work in a full-time capacity at UVM Health – Home Health & Hospice (part-time employees may be eligible dependent upon grant funding availability).
  • Have a “favorable performance” employee rating.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many work hours are required during the program?

Students must maintain their committed work hours upon acceptance into this program.

How are the classes paid for?

UVM Health – HHH will pay CCV directly for the costs of courses for approved participants. Courses that students may partake in must be related to furthering their healthcare careers. Specific courses will be determined on a case-by-case basis with students related to both academic readiness and need as well as future goals.

What if I’m not an employee at UVM Health – Home Health & Hospice? Can I still apply?

Being a full-time employee at HHH is a requirement for application and enrollment.

Interested in joining our team?

Call 800-722-9922 and choose option 3 to talk with one of our talent acquisition partners about full-time job opportunities at HHH.

What if I’m per diem or part-time? Am I eligible?

To be eligible, you need to be a full-time employee. Part-time employees may be eligible to participate depending on grant-funding availability. Priority will be given to full-time employees.

I’ve taken college coursework in the past. Do I need to retake any courses?

Possibly, depending on the timeframe and your grades. A dedicated CCV advisor will review your academic history and work with you to determine the best path to meet your educational goals in healthcare.
