Hiring Fraud Alert!

We have become aware of individuals impersonating Dana-Farber Cancer Institute recruiters to obtain your personal information for fraudulent purposes. This fraudulent activity may include actions such as requesting an interview on a messenger or texting platform(such as WhatsApp), presenting offers for jobs that are unfamiliar to you, asking you to upload personal information and documents, or asking you to deposit checks or withdraw funds. Dana-Farber Cancer Institute takes this very seriously. We will never ask you to send money, under any circumstances. Our recruiters will never use a personal email domain (such as yahoo or gmail) and will ONLY communicate from an email address with the Dana-Farber Cancer Institute domain of dfci.harvard.edu or through our official TextRecruit function to the phone number you provided to us. If you suspect a communication that you have received may be suspicious or fraudulent, please contact your local authorities.
