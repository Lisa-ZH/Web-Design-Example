Current Fellows

Since 1977, Houston Methodist Administrative Fellowship Program has offered a select group of outstanding people an opportunity to participate in health care at the highest levels. Nearly 60 alumni of the program have gone on to become leaders in their chosen fields.

For further information about the fellowship experience, please contact:

  • 2019 – Texas A&M University, Healthcare Administration, M.H.A.
  • 2012 – Texas A&M University, Health, B.S.

Jared Cosper is from Houston, Texas and received a master’s degree in Healthcare Administration and a bachelor’s degree in Health, both from Texas A&M University. Jared’s health care career began more than 18 years ago in South Texas as a youth volunteer for the local EMS agency. While completing his undergraduate studies at Texas A&M University, he worked as a Paramedic and quickly rose to leadership roles. Prior to joining Houston Methodist, Jared served as the Chief of an award-winning EMS system at Montgomery County Hospital District. During his time with Montgomery County, Jared led a team of approximately 300 employees while simultaneously completing his master’s degree. Under his leadership, the organization was rated #1 in customer experience in the United States for four consecutive years (2012 – 2018). Jared joins the Houston Methodist Administrative Fellowship program eager to expand the reach of his leadership with a focus on hospital operations, physician engagement, and continuing to optimize the patient experience. He is excited to join Houston Methodist because of its unrivaled commitment to excellence, safety, service, and innovation. In his personal time, Jared is an outdoor enthusiast who enjoys camping, scuba diving and aviation. 

CONTACT INFO:

ph: 832-986-3839
email: jcosper@houstonmethodist.org
  • 2019 – Louisiana State University- Shreveport, Healthcare Administration, M.H.A.
  • 2017 – Texas State University, Healthcare Administration, B.S.

Jefferson Alegria was born and raised in Houston, TX, and earned his bachelor’s degree in Healthcare Administration from Texas State University. During his time at Texas State, he completed an administrative internship with Houston Methodist Sugar Land Hospital in their Human Resources department. There, he designed an HR/Leader Rounding initiative to improve retention and new employee satisfaction as well as worked on improving the Graduate Nurse internship recruitment and selection process. After graduation, Jefferson was offered an HR Coordinator position with the Houston Methodist Physician Organization. In this role, he on boarded and hired over 200 physicians and their clinical staff, while also facilitating Pathway to Unparalleled, a system-wide employee engagement initiative. While working at Houston Methodist, Jefferson earned his master’s degree in Health Administration from Louisiana State University- Shreveport. Jefferson has a passion for improving the health care system through employee engagement and patient-centered care. He looks forward to expanding his knowledge of hospital operations, practice management, and global health care services under the strong leadership team of Houston Methodist. Outside of work, Jefferson enjoys going to the river and spending quality time with friends, family and his dog.

CONTACT INFO:

ph: 832-986-3967
email: jalegria@houstonmethodist.org
  • 2020 – Xavier University, Health Services Administration, M.H.S.A.
  • 2017 – University of Kentucky, Finance, B.B.A.

Nicholas Bryant was born in Hinesville, Georgia, and spent his childhood in Louisville, Kentucky. He received his bachelor's degree in Finance from The University of Kentucky and will receive his master's degree in Health Services Administration from Xavier University upon the completion of his fellowship. During the didactic portion of his master's program, Nicholas completed a year-long internship at TriHealth in Cincinnati, Ohio where he served as a corporate administrative intern managing projects throughout the system. Most notably, he worked with Perioperative Services creating a database to locate surgical equipment and developed a business plan for a prospective vendor that would be providing surgical lasers in each hospital. Additionally, his duties included reporting to the directors of each department, auditing the donations of the health systems fundraising campaign, analyzing the physician contract database, and organizing emergency drills for joint commission requirements. During his fellowship at Houston Methodist, Nicholas hopes to gain experience in hospital finance, operations, and leadership development. His dedication to health care is rooted in his passion for improving the care delivered to those most in need and increasing the access to exceptional care for every member of the community. Outside of work, Nicholas enjoys traveling, listening to and playing music, and spending time with friends and family, along with being a self-proclaimed foodie.

email: nbryant@houstonmethodist.org
