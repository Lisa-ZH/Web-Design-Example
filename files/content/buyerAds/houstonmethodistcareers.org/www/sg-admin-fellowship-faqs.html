Houston Methodist Administrative Fellowship
Frequently Asked Questions

Do you accept individuals who have already earned their Master’s degree and are not currently an academic student?

Yes, we do.

Do you accept individuals who have not completed the academic portion of their Master’s degree?

No, applicants must complete the didactic portion of their degree in order to be eligible for the Administrative Fellowship Programs.

Do you accept individuals who have completed the didactic portion of their Master’s degree, but have not officially graduated?

Yes, we do. As long as you have completed the didactic (classroom) portion of your Master’s degree before July 1, 2026, you are eligible to apply.

Are International applicants eligible to apply to the Fellowship programs?

We are unable to offer sponsorship to international applicants interested in the Administrative Fellowship Program at Houston Methodist.

Will you notify me if my application is complete/incomplete?

We will notify you via email once your application is complete. This notification will be provided within five business days. We will not notify you if your application is incomplete. Incomplete applications will not be considered.

What if I cannot get an electronic copy of my transcript?

If your school does not provide electronic transcript, then we will accept an official copy of your transcript.

Do you accept International transcripts?

Yes, however International transcripts must be translated to the US equivalent by a certified organization.

Will you accept electronic letters of recommendation?

Yes, scanned letters of recommendation are preferred. All letters of recommendation must be signed.

How many fellows does Houston Methodist accept each year?

Houston Methodist typically selects one to three administrative fellows for the administrative fellowship. This varies based on the applicant pool and the caliber of the candidates.

How long is the Houston Methodist Administrative Fellowship Program?

The fellowship program is 18 months in length.

What types of projects do fellows complete during their fellowship?

It varies with each fellow’s experience. Fellows complete a mix of long-term projects and short-term projects in order to give them the opportunity to work on a project from beginning to end.

What is the salary for a fellow?

Fellows are provided a competitive salary and full benefits for 18 months. A relocation package may also be available.

How well known is the fellowship throughout the organization?

The Houston Methodist Administrative Fellowship has been in existence since 1971. The fellowship is very well known throughout the organization and senior leaders actively welcome fellows to join them in meetings and collaborate on projects. In fact, many of our executives are former fellows and are strongly committed to the fellowship program or the value a fellowship has on a career.

What kinds of jobs can fellows expect to get after they complete their fellowship?

This truly depends on a myriad of factors such as the qualifications and experiences that the fellow had before the fellowship, the positions available, and the career path that the fellow has determined that he/she wants to pursue. The preceptor and CEOs work with fellows to identify opportunities available post-fellowship. Some examples of post-fellowship positions include project manager, director, and administrative director.

Do fellows typically attend conferences? Are they reimbursed?

Fellows have the opportunity to be reimbursed for two health care conferences of choice during their 18-month fellowship.

Is mentoring and professional support provided for fellows?

Fellows receive support from their preceptor, senior advisors, past fellows, and numerous other managers throughout the organization. They provide personal guidance in the fellowship experience as well as in career selection.
